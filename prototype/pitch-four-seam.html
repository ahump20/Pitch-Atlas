Grip Lab

Hold it like this

Lead with the hand. The grip a human can actually hold comes first; the physics tucks in behind it. Loose in the fingertips, never squeezed.

Lay your index and middle fingers flat across the wide part of the horseshoe seam, the part that faces away from you.

Keep them about a finger-width apart. Pressed close is fine; just do not spread them wide.

Rest your thumb underneath on smooth leather, centered below the two fingers.

Hold it out toward the fingertips with a little daylight to the palm, and stay loose.

What it should feel like
Loose in the
fingertips

Let it come off the pads clean. The index and middle pads roll backspin off the top of the ball. A little daylight stays between ball and palm so it leaves clean.

Release Room

Four seams a turn

Thrown over the top with the fingers behind the ball. The release rolls backspin off the fingertips so all four seams cross the oncoming air each revolution. That is the name: four seams biting the airflow per turn.

Paraphrased from the cited reference, not copied. Wikipedia (Four-seam fastball) and eFastball corroborate the cross-seam orientation.

Movement

It rides.
It never rises.

A pitch is defined by its spin axis, so the axis is authored and the Magnus force is computed from it, not hand-placed. Near-horizontal backspin throws that force straight up. The one teaching sentence

Backspin throws a Magnus force upward, but it stays smaller than the ball weight, so the pitch drops less than a spinless one. It rides. It does not rise.

Kagan, citing Nathan: about 0.28 lb of Magnus force against about 0.32 lb of ball weight · The Hardball Times · reputable analysis
